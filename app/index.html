How it Works

Vector is a 3-year program. Applicants to Vector apply alongside their college applications.

1. High School Junior

Candidates must complete key coursework so that they are prepared for the engineering workplace.

  • AP Calc BC
  • AP Chemistry
  • AP Physics C
  • AP English Language & Composition
  • PLTW Principles of Engineering OR Vector Introduction to Engineering
  • SAT (March session recommended)

2. High School Senior

Candidates apply to Vector and interview with Vector Companies. Candidates then rank their favorite companies. Candidates may provide two rankings: 1) Optional early admission - any company which the candidate would prefer over their early admission college application and 2) Regular admission - all companies which candidates would prefer over a traditional College of Engineering education.

  • Repeat SAT if needed (October session)
  • AP Computer Science A
  • PLTW Engineering Design and Development OR Vector Engineering Design I
  • Apply to Vector (November 1st)
  • Interview at Vector Companies (November)
  • Early Admission rankings due (Dec 1st)
  • Last day for early admission offers (December 10th)
  • Regular Admission rankings due (December 21st)
  • Regular Admission Day (January 2nd)
  • Graduate High School!

3. Junior Apprentice I

The first year of training experience. Apprenctices assume a junior role on the engineering team and determine their preferred concentraion.

  • Vector Engineering Design II
  • Vector Linear Algebra
  • Vector Elective (bio, civil, electrical, energy, mechanical, materials)
  • 40 hours/week junior apprenticeship. $18,000 stipend.
  • 4 weeks vacation
  • Declare Concentraion

4. Junior Apprentice II

Apprentices assume graduated responsibility within their chosen concentration.

  • 16 semester hours of Vector curriculum in Apprentice's concentration
  • 40 hours/week junior apprenticeship. $22,000 stipend.
  • 4 weeks vacation

5. Senior Apprentice

Apprentices progress towards the responsibilities of a full-time Junior Engineer.

  • 12 semester hours of Vector curriculum in Apprentice's concentration
  • Take Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) examination in California
  • 5 paid days leave for job interviews through Vector Career Portal
  • 40 hours/week junior apprenticeship. $25,000 stipend.
  • 4 weeks vacation
  • Graduate Vector! Begin full-time work as a Junior Engineer
