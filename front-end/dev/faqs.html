Frequently Asked Questions

Prerequisites

  • What are the required prerequisites to apply to Vector?

    In order to be prepared for the Junior Apprentice role, applicants must complete the following AP classes at some point:

    • AP Calc BC
    • AP Physics C
    • AP Chemistry
    • AP English Language & Composition
    • AP Computer Science A

    Applicants must also complete the following courses through the PLTW curriculum OR through Vector’s equivalent program:

    • PLTW Principles of Engineering OR Vector Introduction to Engineering (recommend taking in Junior Year)
    • PLTW Engineering Design and Development OR Vector Engineering Design I (recommend taking in Senior Year)

  • Do I need to take AP classes or can I just take the exams?

    We do not require you to have taken the AP classes. The scores from AP exams will suffice as we do not want to exclude homeschooled students or students from schools that don’t offer AP classes.

  • When should I take my required AP exams?

    We would like to see scores from 4 out of the 5 required AP classes. Take 4 of the 5 courses during your junior year of high school and take the last one in your senior year.

  • My school doesn’t offer the PLTW curriculum. What do I do?

    Vector offers equivalent courses. You could take those instead. There is a fee of $5/month to take these courses, but this will be deducted from your application fee.

  • Do I need to take the SAT?

    Yes. We recommend the March session of your Junior year. If you need to retake your SAT, aim for the October session of your Senior year.

Application process

  • Is there an application fee?

    Yes. Vector charges a $50 application fee. If you took Vector Introduction to Engineering this fee will be waived.

  • What is the application deadline? When are interviews?

    The deadline to submit an application is November 1st of your Senior Year. Interviews will be conducted throughout November.

  • When do I find out if I matched at a company?

    Vector offers both early and regular admissions. You can participate in both.

    Early admission rankings are due December 1st. Early admission offers will be released by December 10th.

    Regular admission rankings are due December 21st. Regular admission offers with be released on January 2nd.

  • What is early admission? Does Vector early admission conflict with my regular college early admission?

    Not really. Early admission is designed to let you know if you got into Vector before you find out about your college early admission results. This gives you time to withdraw your college application without a penalty.

    Only apply to Vector early admission companies if you would prefer to work at those companies over your top college choice.

  • I like some of the Vector Companies but don’t like others. Can I pick and choose from the companies I like?

    Yes. As part of your application, you will select from the list of Vector Companies. You can select anywhere from one to all the companies. You will only be considered for an interview at the companies in which you’ve expressed interest.

  • I am interested in X type of Engineering, but a Company is mostly a not-X type of Engineering company. Should I apply?

    No. Only pick companies that do work that interests you. Doing something you are not interested in will be bad for both you and the company.

  • What happens after I submit my application?

    Vector will interview the top 25% of applicants. Of these applicants, we will select approximately 20% and forward them to be interviewed by the companies in which the applicant expressed interest.

  • Do I have to travel for interviews?

    No. Both Vector and the Vector Companies will conduct phone/video interviews.

  • What should I do after I interview?

    After you complete all your interviews, you will rank the companies based on your order of preferences. Companies will do the same. An algorithm will match applicants to companies who ranked each other highly.

  • Should I rank a company if I don’t want to end up there?

    No. Only rank a company if you want to work there. It’s better to not get an offer at all than to get an offer you don’t want.

  • Do I have to go to to the company I matched at?

    Yes. Please do not rank a company at which you’d be unhappy working. A penalty may be assessed in this situation.

Salary & Expenses

  • What is the salary?

    • Junior Apprentice I: $18,000 Stipend.
    • Junior Apprentice II: $22,000 Stipend.
    • Senior Apprentice: $25,000 Stipend.

    The salary is meant to cover all living expenses including housing, food, transportation and entertainment.

  • Does Vector provide housing?

    Vector does provide optional housing accommodations at some of its locations. We are working to expand the housing locations based on student demand. If we don’t have housing accommodations at your particular training site, you will have to find an alternative arrangement.

  • Do I get health insurance?

    You can get health insurance through Vector Benefits.

  • Does Vector cover my transportation to my training site?

    No. Transportation is not covered, however you may be able to get tax deductions through Vector Benefits.

  • Does Vector pay for my FE exam?

    No. Budget for this in your Senior Apprentice year.

  • Do I get vacation?

    Yes. You get 4 weeks of vacation per year. You also get 5 days for job interviews in your Senior Apprentice year.

The Vector Curriculum

  • How many hours per week should I spend learning the coursework?

    We recommend 20 hours per week to work through all the material.

  • Do I have to watch all the classes and do all the problems?

    No. Our only requirement is that you pass all your learning assessments. If you can do that without watching any of the classes, that’s fine with us.

  • Are there exams? What if I fail?

    There are learning assessments. You must pass all these eventually. If you fail, you can try again. There are no classes during the summer months, so that is also a good time to catch up if you’re behind.

  • What are the first year courses?

    In your Junior Apprentice I year you will take Linear Algebra, Vector Engineering Design II and an elective (choose from Bio, Civil, Electrical, Energy, Mechanical, Materials).

  • What are the courses in the second and third years?

    In your Junior Apprentice II and Senior Apprentice years all your courses will be related to your concentration. The course load is the equivalent of 16 semester hours in college.

Miscellaneous questions

  • Is Vector available to international applicants?

    Not at this time.

  • Can I really become a Professional Engineer without a college degree?

    You can become a Professional Engineer through work experience in the following states: Arizona, California, Georgia, Hawaii, Indiana, Maryland, New Hampshire, New York, Pennsylvania, Vermont and Washington State.

    If you have your heart set on becoming a Professional Engineer in a different state, you should consider the traditional college path (for now). There are also a lot of engineering jobs that don't require a Professional Engineer certificate.

  • How does Vector make money?

    Companies pay us a lump sum every year for each applicant that is matched with them.

  • Who do I list as my employer on government documents?

    You are technically employed by Vector Training Academy Inc.
